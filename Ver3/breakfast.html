The menu

Ground floor of the Twenty Six building.

Breakfast is served in the Twenty Six restaurant downstairs — a room that functions as a living room for District VI. Hungarian produce from the markets at Hunyadi tér, bread baked at dawn, a menu that shifts with the seasons.

We don't do buffets. You order, you sit, you talk. Eggs cooked by someone who cares. The coffee is Onyx. The orange juice is pressed in front of you.

Breakfast is €18 per person, ordered à la carte. If you need an early start, tell us the night before and we'll arrange it.

From Hunyadi tér market.

The market at Hunyadi tér has been selling Budapest its produce since the 1890s. We're regulars. The team shops there each morning for whatever's best that day — which is why the menu shifts slightly with the season, and why the fruit tastes like it was picked recently (because it was).

We believe in knowing where things come from. The suppliers below are the people we've worked with long enough to trust.
